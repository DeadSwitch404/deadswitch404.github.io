ZFS And FreeBSD - Make Difference


You're not better if you use FreeBSD.
You're not more if you have ZFS.
You're different.

FreeBSD isn't just a kernel. It's an OS.

You get a system.
You get a toolset.
You get a mindset.

FreeBSD is a living body.

Documentation

FreeBSD isn't just an OS.
Every release comes with documentation.
With a strong signal.
It's the DNA. The blueprint.

The FreeBSD Handbook is a legendary source of information.
A tool for the beginner, and a blade for the experienced.

Clarity comes without noise.

rc.d

The mindset. The nerves that run deep in the system's spine.
FreeBSD has unique nerves: rc.d.

Minimal abstractions.
No theories.
No guesses.

rc.d doesn't hide any information.
The boot and service start are clear.

You can see it. Modify it. Replicate it. 

ls -al /etc/rc.d/

Shell scripts. /bin/sh and no less, no more.

Plain elegance.

pf

FreeBSD's packet filter.
Trusted by heavy tools like pfSense.

It's the immune system of the OS.

It's part of the kernel.
Userland tools expose the control layer.
You don't edit rules. You paint art in pf.conf.

Ports

Packages and ports are the heart of the OS.

The pkg package manager is a sharp blade.
Some operators prefer to roll back the sleeves: compile.

It's the surgical knife of the operators.

FreeBSD's port collection is source code.
Waiting for compilation.
Fresh. Precise.

A structured tree of Makefiles and patches added.

Jails

FreeBSD jails are extended limbs of the OS.

Jails aren't application containers.
Jails aren't virtual machines.

Jails are isolated, lightweight, breathing systems.

They can have their own network stack.
Own routing.
Own pf rules.

With ZFS - jails are lightweight clones of a template.

The architecture becomes simple and structured.

ZFS

OpenZFS is integrated. Embedded.
From the moment of installation.
It's the bone. The skeleton. The frame.

It's not a filesystem. It's not a volume manager.
It's both - and more.

Mirroring. Raid-Z.
Compression. Encryption.

Auto-mounting with the mountpoint property.

Snapshots reduce the fear of change.

Final Whispers

It's not a competition.
Linux has its place. Windows has its use case.

FreeBSD claims an own place as well.

Where architecture weights.
Where simplicity matters.

FreeBSD isn't loud.
FreeBSD speaks in whispers.
